# Hotel Reservation Cancellation Analysis

This project focuses on analyzing hotel booking data to understand reservation cancellation behavior and identify the factors that contribute to booking cancellations. The analysis aims to provide valuable insights that can help hotels improve customer retention and optimize revenue management strategies.

## Objectives
1. Analyze hotel booking and cancellation trends.
2. Identify major factors influencing reservation cancellations.
3. Examine the impact of pricing, seasonality, and booking channels.
4. Generate actionable business recommendations to reduce cancellation rates.

## Key Questions
1. What factors influence hotel reservation cancellations?
2. How can cancellation rates be reduced?
3. How can hotels optimize pricing and promotions?

## Dataset
The dataset contains hotel booking records from City Hotel and Resort Hotel between July 2015 and August 2017. It includes information related to customer demographics, reservation details, room pricing, booking channels, and cancellation status.

## Tools & Libraries
- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

## Key Findings
- **Pricing Impact**: Reservations with higher room rates showed a greater likelihood of cancellation.
- **Seasonal Trends**: Booking volumes peaked in August, while January recorded the highest cancellation activity.
- **Booking Channels**: Online Travel Agencies accounted for the largest share of reservations (46%), followed by Groups (27%) and Direct bookings (4%).
- **Regional Insights**: Portugal contributed the highest number of canceled reservations among all countries.

## Recommendations
1. Adopt flexible pricing strategies and targeted discounts during high demand periods.
2. Increase promotional and customer engagement efforts during January to address elevated cancellation rates.
3. Focus on improving customer experience and retention initiatives in regions with high cancellation volumes, particularly Portugal.
4. Expand and promote direct booking options to reduce reliance on third party booking platforms.
